## 1. The Idea

### One Sentence

> **Ecomai gives every small business in Bangladesh a professional online store, built-in payments, delivery logistics, and marketing tools — and we earn a commission on every sale they make.**

### The Thesis

There are **7.8 million small and medium businesses** in Bangladesh. Over **500,000** of them are already trying to sell products through Facebook pages, WhatsApp groups, and personal bKash accounts. They're running real businesses with makeshift tools.

They don't need a basic website. They need a **complete commerce operating system** — one that handles their storefront, payments, inventory, deliveries, invoicing, customer management, and marketing — **all from a single dashboard they can open on their phone.**

That's Ecomai.

We don't just build them a store. We become the invisible infrastructure behind their entire business. And every time a customer pays online through their store, **we earn a percentage.** The more they sell, the more we earn. Our success is mathematically tied to theirs.

### The Core Insight

The insight that drives everything:

> **In emerging markets, commerce infrastructure doesn't exist yet. Whoever builds it first, owns it.**

Shopify did this for the Western world. Daraz (Alibaba) tried through a marketplace model. But no one has built a **self-serve, localized, commission-earning platform** for the Bangladeshi entrepreneur who sells clothing on Facebook and collects money via personal bKash.

**That's the gap. We fill it.**

## 2. Why Now — The Market Moment

Five forces are converging to create a once-in-a-decade window for Ecomai:

### Force 1: Digital Payment Explosion

Bangladesh's Mobile Financial Services (MFS) crossed **৳1.2 Trillion in monthly transactions** in 2025. bKash alone has 75+ million registered users. Nagad crossed 60 million. For the first time, the *infrastructure* for digital commerce is mature enough for mass adoption.

### Force 2: The F-Commerce Wave

An estimated **500,000+ small sellers** operate primarily through Facebook pages in Bangladesh. They use:
- Facebook Messenger for orders
- Personal bKash/Nagad for payments
- Handwritten notebooks for inventory
- WhatsApp groups for delivery coordination

They're doing ৳50,000 to ৳500,000 per month in revenue — but running a *professional* business with *amateur* tools. They're ready for an upgrade. They just need it to be easy, cheap, and in their language.

### Force 3: Government Push for Digital Commerce

The Bangladesh government's **"Digital Bangladesh"** and **"Smart Bangladesh"** initiatives are actively encouraging SME digitization. BIDA (Bangladesh Investment Development Authority) and the SME Foundation have active programs and budgets to support e-commerce enablers.

### Force 4: COVID Behavioral Shift (Permanent)

The pandemic permanently shifted consumer expectations. Online ordering, digital payments, and delivery expectations are no longer novel — they're expected. The businesses that don't go digital will lose customers to those that do.

### Force 5: AI Is Ready

For the first time, AI can generate product descriptions, marketing copy, website designs, and business analytics at near-zero marginal cost. This is an incredible lever for small businesses that can't afford a marketing team or a web designer. **We can provide a ৳500,000-worth service for ৳500** using AI.

## 4. The Business Model — How We Make Money

Ecomai employs a **triple-layer monetization model.** Each layer serves a different strategic purpose.

### Layer 1: Subscriptions — The Foundation

Predictable, recurring revenue. This covers our operating costs.

| Plan | Monthly Price | What They Get |
|---|---|---|
| **Free** | ৳0 | 10 products, 50 orders/month, basic store |
| **Starter** | ৳999 (~$9) | 100 products, 500 orders, online payments, delivery tools |
| **Growth** | ৳2,499 (~$23) | 1,000 products, 5,000 orders, marketing, invoicing, analytics |
| **Enterprise** | ৳4,999 (~$46) | Unlimited everything, custom domain, priority support |

**Why a free tier?** Because the biggest barrier in Bangladesh isn't price — it's **trust.** A free tier lets sellers try us with zero risk. Once they see sales coming in, they upgrade.

**Yearly billing:** 20% discount. Incentivizes long-term commitment, improves our cash flow predictability.

### Layer 2: Transaction Commission — The Growth Engine

**This is the core of the model.** Every time a customer pays online through a shop on our platform, we automatically take a percentage.

| Subscription Plan | Commission Rate | On a ৳10,000 Sale |
|---|---|---|
| Free | 8% | ৳800 → Ecomai |
| Starter | 5% | ৳500 → Ecomai |
| Growth | 3% | ৳300 → Ecomai |
| Enterprise | 1.5% | ৳150 → Ecomai |

**Why this is brilliant:**

1. **Aligned incentives.** We only earn when our shops earn. We're motivated to help them sell more.
2. **Automatic upgrade path.** As shops grow, they upgrade to lower commission plans — but they're generating so much more volume that our absolute revenue per shop increases.
3. **Scales infinitely.** Whether we have 100 shops or 100,000 shops, commission revenue scales linearly with GMV.
4. **Only on online payments.** COD is excluded. This incentivizes shops to push digital payments (which benefits the whole ecosystem).

**The key insight:** A shop on the Free plan doing ৳100,000/month in online sales generates **৳8,000/month in commission** for us — even though they pay ৳0 in subscription. In many cases, commission revenue per shop will exceed subscription revenue.

### Layer 3: Value-Added Services — The Multiplier

Future premium features that shops will pay for on top of their plan:

| Service | Model | Why It Works |
|---|---|---|
| **AI Website Generation** | ৳500-2,000/use | Shop owners can't design; AI does it for them |
| **AI Marketing Tools** | ৳200/campaign | Most shops can't write marketing copy |
| **Premium Themes** | ৳1,000-5,000 one-time | Visual differentiation |
| **SMS/WhatsApp Notifications** | Per-message | Customer communication is critical |
| **Featured Listings** | ৳500-5,000/week | When we launch marketplace discovery |
| **Advanced Analytics** | ৳999/mo add-on | For data-driven shops |

### The Math — Revenue Per Shop

A typical Starter-plan shop doing ৳150,000/mo in GMV with 40% online payments:


### What About Cash-on-Delivery?

COD orders flow through the system for tracking and management — but **no commission is charged.** The money goes directly from customer to delivery driver to shop owner. It never touches the platform.

**Why this is smart:**
- COD is ~70% of orders in Bangladesh. Charging commission on it would be unenforceable and would alienate merchants.
- By making online payment the *only* path where commission is charged, we create a natural incentive for shops to push customers toward digital payment.
- As digital payment adoption grows (30% → 50% → 70% over 5 years), our commission revenue automatically increases — even without acquiring new shops.

### The Withdrawal Lifecycle

Shop owners don't just see numbers — they can **withdraw real money** to their real accounts.

```
  Shop owner has ৳50,000 balance
           │
           ▼
  Requests withdrawal of ৳30,000 to bKash
           │
           ▼
  ┌─── VALIDATION ───┐
  │ Balance sufficient│
  │ Above min (৳500) │
  │ No pending request│
  └────────┬─────────┘
           │
           ▼
  ┌─── PENDING ──────┐
  │ Super Admin sees  │
  │ request in queue  │
  └────────┬─────────┘
           │
     ┌─────┴──────┐
     ▼            ▼
  APPROVED     REJECTED
     │         (reason given)
     ▼
  PROCESSING
  (sending via bKash)
     │
     ▼
  COMPLETED ─── ৳30,000 sent, balance now ৳20,000
```

**Why manual approval matters:**
- Fraud prevention (new shops requesting large withdrawals)
- Cash flow management for the platform
- Creates a relationship touchpoint with merchants
- Can be automated for trusted, established shops in the future

### Super Admin — The Control Tower

The person running Ecomai (the super admin) sees **everything**:

| Capability | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **Platform-wide earnings dashboard** | Total GMV, total commission collected, across all shops |
| **Per-shop balance view** | How much each shop has earned, withdrawn, and still holds |
| **Withdrawal queue** | Every pending withdrawal request, with one-click approve/reject |
| **Commission configuration** | Set global rate (5%) or override per-shop (reward top sellers) |
| **Manual adjustments** | Credit or debit any shop's balance (refunds, bonuses, corrections) with mandatory logging |
| **Minimum withdrawal threshold** | Prevent micro-withdrawals that cost more to process than they're worth |
| **Payout scheduling** | Set withdrawal cycles: on-request, weekly, bi-weekly, monthly |

This isn't just accounting — this is the **business intelligence layer** that lets us make strategic decisions in real-time.

Every day a shop uses Ecomai, they:
- Build up customer profiles and order history
- Accumulate earnings and withdrawal records
- Create marketing campaign history
- Store product catalog and inventory data

**This data doesn't transfer.** The longer they stay, the harder it is to leave. This is the same moat that makes Salesforce, QuickBooks, and Shopify so sticky.

### The Network Effect (Future)

As more shops join Ecomai:
- We can launch a **marketplace/discovery feature** where consumers browse across shops
- More traffic for everyone → more sales → more commission
- Suppliers want to be listed where the sellers are
- Delivery partners integrate because that's where the volume is

**This is a classic platform network effect.** Each new participant makes the platform more valuable for everyone else.
